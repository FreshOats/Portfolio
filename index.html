Recent Projects

I Think I've Been Poisoned...

An investigation of chemical contaminants in Califonia's drinking water, this project uses data from the California Open Data portal in conjunction with safe levels of such contaminants in the water. The data were about as clean as the water in Hinkley, CA in the early 1990s, so Following data cleaning, the contamination levels were compared to government-set safety guidelines as well as CDC set dangerous guidelines. The results are presented on an interactive Tableau story, looking at levels within the past 5 years and throughout the state's history, available on Tableau Public. Considering the nature of the data presented, solutions and resources for people potentially affected have been provided within the story.

NFL Injury Analytics

In recent years, the gravity of consequences due to injuries sustained in the NFL has increaesed the need for research on factors leading to these injuries so that the NFL can take preventative measures to protect the players, while still maintaining the features of American Football. This project explores the data for patterns and correlations for lower-body injuries as well as concussions, using statistical analytics as part of the feature analysis as well as employs machine learning and deep learning to try to better identify the predictors associated with the types and severities of the injuries.

A/B Testing and Analysis of an Online Marketing Campaign

A large company has implemented a new Ad campaign and wants to know whether the new ad can increase conversion by at least 2%, as well as information about the ideal times and how many ads to run per hour. This analysis utilizes a sample of 20,000 users that have been split 60:40 for Ad:Control, and assesses the distributions, relationships, and statistics of whether the campaign is significant and what other insights can be offered to the company if they decide to launch the campaign.

Bayesian Marketing Analysis

This uses Bayesian Statistics to analyze the findings of an A/B test from a marketing campaign, incorporating a Monte Carlo simulation to reduce sampling error. In addition to showing that the test outperforms the control, it further shows how much better the test performed, and the likeliness that it will perform at that level.
